Does My Medical Condition Qualify? Who qualifies for medical marijuana treatment? New York State's Medical Marijuana Law makes patients, of any age, eligible to use medical marijuana if they have been diagnosed by a Licensed Provider as having a specific severe, debilitating or life-threatening condition accompanied by an associated or complicating condition. Conditions currently approved as qualifying for a medical marijuana prescription are as follows: Cancer HIV/AIDS Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) Parkinson’s disease Multiple sclerosis Spinal cord nerve injury with intractable spasticity Epilepsy Inflammatory bowel disease Neuropathy Huntington’s disease Chronic Pain (effective as of 3/22/2017) - https://health.ny.gov/press/releases/2016/2016-12-01_chronic_pain_condition_added.htm The associated or complicating conditions: cachexia or wasting syndrome severe or chronic pain severe nausea seizures severe or persistent muscle spasms Read more on the NYS Health Department's website. https://www.health.ny.gov/regulations/medical_marijuana/patients/
